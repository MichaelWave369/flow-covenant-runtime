# Worked Examples

These examples show how Flow Covenant Runtime can be used without turning metaphor into proof or bypassing accountability.

## 1. AI collaboration

**Scenario:** A user wants to publish a poetic idea as a strong public claim.

**Signal:** The idea is meaningful, but claim-risk is present.

**Invitation:** Develop the idea as philosophy, systems design, or hypothesis, not proof.

**Consent:** The author wants public development; readers need a clear boundary.

**Alignment:** Keep beauty and accountability together. Separate metaphor, evidence, assumptions, and unknowns.

**Flow action:** Produce a draft with claim-safe wording.

**Receipt:** Record what was changed, what claims were softened, and what remains unproven.

**Renewal:** Review language after feedback and revise if readers misunderstand the boundary.

## 2. Software workflow

**Scenario:** A team wants to ship a release before rollback and audit receipts are ready.

**Signal:** Speed is creating drift.

**Invitation:** Turn the release into a staged rollout with clear rollback.

**Consent:** Users, maintainers, and support teams are affected.

**Alignment:** Do not settle irreversible changes while gate status is blocked.

**Flow action:** Ship behind a feature flag or sandbox first.

**Receipt:** Save test result, rollout scope, rollback owner, and review time.

**Renewal:** Review support load, errors, and user trust signals.

## 3. Community governance

**Scenario:** A public promise is being discussed, but records are incomplete.

**Signal:** Trust depends on separating measured, promised, claimed, unknown, and disputed items.

**Invitation:** Create a public receipt packet.

**Consent:** Affected residents and institutions need visibility and question paths.

**Alignment:** Avoid authority theater; publish the evidence boundary.

**Flow action:** Ask written questions and request receipts before settlement.

**Receipt:** Record each promise and its evidence class.

**Renewal:** Schedule a review date and publish what changed.

## 4. Personal reflection

**Scenario:** A person feels pressure to act while emotionally charged.

**Signal:** Urgency may narrow choice.

**Invitation:** Choose a pause, temporary boundary, or draft message.

**Consent:** Self and others need non-harming space.

**Alignment:** Preserve truth, relationship, and future repair.

**Flow action:** Delay irreversible words or actions.

**Receipt:** Record what was felt, what was chosen, and what remains unknown.

**Renewal:** Revisit after emotion settles.
