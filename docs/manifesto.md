# Flow Covenant Manifesto

Law says: obey. Flow says: participate.

Law asks: who violated? Flow asks: what drifted, what was harmed, and what needs repair?

Law can become a cage when it forgets life. Flow can become chaos when it forgets boundary.

The covenant is the middle path: play with shape, movement with consent, action with receipt, correction with mercy.

A system becomes alive-feeling when it can notice, invite, listen, align, move, remember, and renew.

This is not an escape from responsibility. It is responsibility made relational.

## Working principles

1. Life is play, not force.
2. Boundaries are instruments, not cages.
3. Coherence is invited, not imposed.
4. Correction is return, not shame.
5. Participation beats compliance.
6. Receipts replace authority theater.
7. Repair keeps the playfield alive.

## Claim-safe boundary

This is a philosophy and systems-design model, not a claim that physical laws literally want, think, or feel.
