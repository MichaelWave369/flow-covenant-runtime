# Flow Covenant Runtime Decision Cards

Use these cards when a group gets stuck, rushes, overclaims, hides power, or forgets repair.

## Card 1 — Name the Signal

**Prompt:** What is actually arising before interpretation, blame, or urgency gets added?

**Use when:** The room is arguing about meaning before naming the event.

## Card 2 — Turn Command into Invitation

**Prompt:** What is being offered, and what would a clean refusal path look like?

**Use when:** The request feels like pressure, compliance, or authority theater.

## Card 3 — Map Affected Parties

**Prompt:** Who or what is affected now, downstream, and after the decision becomes public?

**Use when:** A decision could affect people who are not in the room.

## Card 4 — Separate Evidence from Story

**Prompt:** What is measured, promised, claimed, assumed, unknown, or disputed?

**Use when:** Claims are emotionally strong but records are incomplete.

## Card 5 — Choose the Reversible Move

**Prompt:** What is the smallest pause, draft, test, rollback, or staged action that preserves repair?

**Use when:** The next move may be hard to undo.

## Card 6 — Write the Receipt

**Prompt:** What changed, why, who consented, what remains unknown, and when will we review it?

**Use when:** The group is ready to settle action or close a repair loop.

## Card 7 — Return Without Shame

**Prompt:** What correction would help the system return to coherence without denial or humiliation?

**Use when:** Someone made a mistake and the room is choosing between punishment, avoidance, or repair.

## Card 8 — Keep Beauty and Accountability Together

**Prompt:** Does this path preserve both the spirit of the idea and the evidence boundary?

**Use when:** The idea is inspiring, but the claim language may be drifting too far.
