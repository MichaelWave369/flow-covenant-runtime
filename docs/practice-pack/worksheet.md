# Flow Covenant Runtime Practice Worksheet

Use this worksheet when a decision, conflict, promise, release, or repair needs to move from force-first thinking into coherent participation.

## Claim-safe boundary

This is a philosophy and systems-design model, not a claim that physical laws literally want, think, or feel.

## 1. Scenario

- What is happening?
- What is the decision, tension, request, promise, or drift?
- What domain is this in? AI collaboration, software workflow, community governance, personal reflection, product decision, or other?

## 2. Signal

- What is arising before interpretation or blame?
- What changed?
- What signal is direct evidence?
- What is only assumption or story?

## 3. Invitation

- What is being offered?
- Is this a true invitation or a disguised command?
- What would a clean refusal, delay, or revision path look like?

## 4. Affected parties

- Who is directly affected?
- Who is downstream?
- What systems, records, relationships, or public trust are affected?
- Who needs consent, notice, or representation?

## 6. Repair and reversibility

- What drifted?
- What must be stabilized immediately?
- What can be paused, drafted, sandboxed, staged, reviewed, undone, or rolled back?
- What repair move restores coherence without hiding accountability?
- What follow-up signal proves the field is healthier?

## 7. Receipt

- What action was chosen?
- Why was it chosen?
- Who or what was affected?
- What evidence supports it?
- What assumptions remain?
- What unknowns remain?
- When will this be reviewed?

## Closing principle

Force creates compliance. Flow creates participation.
