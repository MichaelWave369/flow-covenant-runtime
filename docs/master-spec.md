# Flow Covenant Runtime Master Spec v0.1

## Abstract

Flow Covenant Runtime (FCR) is a philosophy-to-systems scaffold that reframes governance, software orchestration, AI collaboration, and human decision workflows away from force-first law thinking and toward participation-first flow thinking.

The model does not reject boundaries, constraints, accountability, evidence, or repair. It repositions them as instruments that shape coherent play rather than cages that demand blind compliance.

## Core thesis

Healthy systems sustain order most effectively when participants can understand the invitation, consent to the relation, move within meaningful boundaries, generate auditable receipts, and renew the field after action.

## Runtime loop

Signal -> Invitation -> Consent -> Alignment -> Flow -> Receipt -> Renewal

## Primary formula

> Force creates compliance. Flow creates participation.

## Non-negotiable boundaries

- No literalized claim that natural laws have human-like desire or consciousness.
- No bypassing consent, evidence, or accountability in the name of flow.
- No shame-based correction model.
- No authoritarian replacement of law with vague charisma.
- No medical, spiritual, scientific, or legal overclaiming.

## Governance gates

1. Consent Gate — Is participation informed and voluntary?
2. Harm Gate — Does this minimize harm and coercion?
3. Claim Gate — Is the claim clear and self-contained?
4. Evidence Gate — What signals, data, or receipts support this?
5. Reversibility Gate — Can this be undone or adjusted if needed?
6. Repair Gate — If disruption occurs, how will we repair?

## Falsifier tests

FCR fails if:

- it makes harm harder to name;
- it hides power instead of exposing it;
- it weakens consent;
- it replaces evidence with vibes;
- it cannot produce a useful receipt.
